Of New York City, For the World

As the Jesuit University of New York, we question assumptions, dig deeper, and rise to the challenges of our times. At Fordham, we're a tight-knit community in New York City, and we value and educate the whole person. We connect the people and communities who shape a more hopeful world.

Fordham in NYC and Beyond

Fordham students can study on our 85-acre Rose Hill campus in the Bronx or in the heart of Manhattan at our Lincoln Center campus. Outside of the city, Fordham London, the Calder Center, and Westchester campus offer opportunities for study abroad, research, and professional studies. If you are not able to make it to campus, take advantage of our online degrees.

Rose Hill

The Bronx, New York City

Our Rose Hill campus, in the north Bronx, is as idyllic as college campuses get: 85 acres featuring Gothic architecture and tree-lined walkways.

Explore Rose Hill

Lincoln Center

Manhattan, New York City

Our Lincoln Center campus, in Midtown Manhattan, allows you to take full advantage of both the city that surrounds us and our welcoming campus.

Explore Lincoln Center

Westchester

West Harrison, New York

Our Westchester campus is located in a three-story, state-of-the-art building on 32 landscaped acres in West Harrison, NY.

Explore Westchester

Louis Calder Center

Armonk, New York

Fordham's biological field station comprises 113 forested acres with a variety of ecosystems and communities available for study.

Explore Calder Center

London

Clerkenwell, London, UK

Fordham London is home to academic programs in business and the liberal arts, along with a robust internship program.

Explore London
