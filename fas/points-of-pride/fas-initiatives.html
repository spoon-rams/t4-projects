A&S Deans’ Anti-Racism Advisory Committee
In response to the injustice taking place in our society and together with the Conference of A&S Deans (CASD), the A&S Deans’ Anti-Racism Advisory Committee was formed in December 2020 after a call for participants was issued amongst faculty and administrators. The Committee held its first meeting in February 2021, with the goal to help CASD develop an anti-racism and social justice action plan for the short- and long term, offering the deans an advisory body on matters relating to our mission to disrupt anti-Black racism and promote racial justice in all facets of the arts and science’ work. The Committee has been hosting bi-monthly meetings with its members (faculty, administrators, and students) for the past two years to address this charge, while supporting  and coordinating departments’ and programs’ work on anti-racism, to keep all parties informed and inspired, and continue to liaise with the Office of the Chief Diversity Officer and the DEI Council. Recent priorities within the committee have included faculty and staff recruitment and retention, rethinking mentorship, and conducting an audit of policies within Arts & Sciences to ensure that they are equitable and inclusive.  The Committee’s Summary of Recommendations can be found here. Questions and suggestions can be sent to the Committee at as_antiracism@fordham.edu.

On February 23 the Department of African and African American Studies held a lecture which featured Khiara M. Bridges, Ph.D., a leading scholar on critical race theory, who reviewed the state of racial bias in American law and society. In her talk, “Critical Race Theory: An Introduction,” Bridges presented the history of the academic concept that has recently been in the news and addressed the misinformation circling the topic. Tyesha Maddox, Ph.D., Assistant Professor of African and African American Studies and the event’s organizer, said the lecture solidified her ideas “of exactly what critical race theory is in a very simple way.” To learn more about the event, please click here.

Additionally, the Department of Communication and Media Studies held an event on February 28 featuring Michele Prettyman, Assistant Professor and co-Chair of Fordham’s A&S Deans’ Advisory Committee on Anti-Racism, in conversation with Charity Elder, an award-winning journalist and media executive, as well as an Adjunct Instructor at Fordham University, to discuss Charity's recent book, Power: The Rise of Black Women in America. Throughout the discussion Charity shared personal stories, shed insight into her experience writing the book, and even recalled some tough moments that proved to be eye opening lessons. Professor Prettyman moderated the discussion and upon reflecting says, "It was a pleasure to talk to and learn from a colleague like Professor Elder. Her book offers a unique perspective on black women's success in America and compelling insights on her own personal and professional journey. These community conversations are a vital part of what we missed during the pandemic!”
